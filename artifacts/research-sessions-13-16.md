**DISCREPANCY FOUND -- Response Scale Numbering:**
The reference document uses a **0-4 scale**, while the published 5-point version (Betz, Hammond, & Multon, 2005) uses a **1-5 scale**. The anchor labels are identical, but the numeric values differ by 1. This affects scoring ranges:

- Reference doc: Subscale sum range 0-20, Total range 0-100
- Published 1-5 version: Subscale sum range 5-25, Total range 25-125

The 0-4 numbering used in the reference document is internally consistent (sum of 25 items at max = 100, which is a clean total), and the anchor labels match the published instrument. This appears to be a deliberate adaptation to simplify scoring, but it departs from the standard 1-5 numbering used in the 2005 revision.

**Note on scale history:** The original 1996 CDSE-SF used a 10-point continuum (0-9). The 5-point version was validated in 2005 by Betz, Hammond, and Multon, who found it at least as reliable (alpha .78-.87) as the 10-level version.

### Item Content Verification

The CDSE-SF is a **copyrighted instrument** (Mind Garden, Inc.), and the full 25-item text is not freely published online. However, based on sample items published in peer-reviewed papers, partial verification is possible:

**DISCREPANCY FOUND -- Item Ordering:**
In the published CDSE-SF, items from the five subscales are **interleaved** (distributed across the questionnaire), not grouped sequentially by subscale. The reference document groups items sequentially (items 1-5 = Self-Appraisal, 6-10 = Occupational Information, etc.). This is a common adaptation for platform-based administration where subscale grouping simplifies the scoring engine, but it differs from the published item ordering. This may also introduce response pattern bias (related items appearing consecutively).

The reference document provides reasonable interpretation bands consistent with how the instrument is typically used in career counseling literature, though the specific cutoff values (80-100 = High, 50-79 = Moderate, etc.) are platform-specific and not from the original publication.

### Verdict: CDSE-SF

- **Overall alignment:** GOOD with noted discrepancies

- **Recommendation:** The 0-4 scale is internally consistent and produces clean scoring ranges. Document the departure from the 1-5 convention. Consider whether sequential item grouping is intentional or should be corrected to interleaved ordering.

## 2. Session 14: ALQ -- Authentic Leadership Self-Assessment Questionnaire

### Source Verification

- **Based on:** Walumbwa, F. O., Avolio, B. J., Gardner, W. L., Wernsing, T. S., & Peterson, S. J. (2008)
- **Published:** Journal of Management, 34(1), 89-126
- **Instrument used in reference doc:** The Northouse "Authentic Leadership Self-Assessment Questionnaire," a self-report adaptation included in Northouse's *Leadership: Theory and Practice* textbook
- **The ALQ proper:** Copyrighted by Avolio, Gardner, & Walumbwa (2007), distributed by Mind Garden. The ALQ is designed to rate a leader (rater version), while the Northouse version is a self-assessment.
- **Reference doc citation:** CONFIRMED correct

**Important distinction:** The reference document uses the **Northouse self-assessment adaptation** (which shares the same theoretical four-factor structure as the ALQ) rather than the proprietary Mind Garden ALQ instrument. Both are 16-item instruments measuring the same four constructs, but with different item wordings. The Northouse version is freely available in textbooks and online.

### Item Count and Subscale Structure

| Attribute | Reference Document | Northouse Self-Assessment | ALQ (Walumbwa) | Match? |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Total items | 16 | 16 | 16 | YES |
| Self-Awareness items | 4 (items 1-4) | 4 (items 1, 5, 9, 13) | 4 | PARTIAL |
| Relational Transparency items | 5 (items 5-9) | 4 (items 4, 8, 12, 16) | 5 | DISCREPANCY |
| Internalized Moral Perspective items | 4 (items 10-13) | 4 (items 2, 6, 10, 14) | 4 | YES |
| Balanced Processing items | 3 (items 14-16) | 4 (items 3, 7, 11, 15) | 3 | DISCREPANCY |

**DISCREPANCY FOUND -- Subscale Item Counts:**
The reference document assigns **5 items to Relational Transparency** and **3 items to Balanced Processing**. This matches the original **ALQ** by Walumbwa et al. (2008), which has 5 RT items and 3 BP items. However, the **Northouse self-assessment** distributes items evenly (4 per subscale). The reference document appears to have hybridized: it uses Northouse-style self-assessment item wordings but applies the ALQ's uneven subscale distribution (4-5-4-3).

### Item Content Comparison

Below is a comparison of the reference document items against the verified Northouse self-assessment items:

|---|---|---|---|---|

| 2 | I can list my three greatest weaknesses. | My actions reflect my core values. (Item 2) | IMP in Northouse | DIFFERENT ITEM |
| 3 | I seek feedback from others to improve how I interact with them. | I seek others' opinions before making up my own mind. (Item 3) | BP in Northouse | DIFFERENT ITEM |

**DISCREPANCY FOUND -- Item Ordering and Wording:**
The reference document groups items sequentially by subscale (items 1-4 = Self-Awareness, 5-9 = Relational Transparency, 10-13 = IMP, 14-16 = BP), while the Northouse version interleaves items across subscales (items 1,5,9,13 = SA; 2,6,10,14 = IMP; etc.). However, the individual item *texts* in the reference document do match items found in the Northouse questionnaire, just in different order and subscale grouping. Specifically:

**Reference Document Self-Awareness items (1-4) mapped to Northouse:**
- "I can list my three greatest strengths" = Northouse Item 5 (SA)
- "I can list my three greatest weaknesses" = Northouse Item 1 (SA)
- "I seek feedback from others to improve how I interact with them" = similar to Northouse Item 9 ("I seek feedback as a way of understanding who I really am as a person") -- slightly reworded
- "I accept the feelings I have about myself" = Northouse Item 13 (SA)

**Reference Document Relational Transparency items (5-9) mapped to Northouse:**
- "I say exactly what I mean" = Not a direct match to any Northouse item (potential addition)
- "I admit my mistakes to others" = Northouse Item 16 (RT)
- "I openly share my feelings with others" = Northouse Item 4 (RT)
- "I let others know who I truly am as a person" = Northouse Item 8 (RT)
- "I tell the truth even when it is not easy" = Not a direct Northouse match (potential addition)

**Reference Document IMP items (10-13) mapped to Northouse:**
- "I show consistency between my beliefs and actions" = similar to Northouse Item 2 ("My actions reflect my core values")
- "I make decisions based on my core values" = similar to Northouse Item 14 ("My morals guide what I do as a leader")
- "I resist pressure to do things contrary to my beliefs" = similar to Northouse Item 6 ("I do not allow group pressure to control me")
- "I am guided by ethical principles in my decision-making" = Not a direct Northouse match

**Reference Document BP items (14-16) mapped to Northouse:**
- "I listen carefully to different viewpoints before reaching a conclusion" = similar to Northouse Item 15 ("I listen very carefully to the ideas of others before making decisions")
- "I seek out information that challenges my own position" = similar to Northouse Item 7 ("I listen closely to the ideas of those who disagree with me")
- "I consider other people's perspectives before making decisions" = similar to Northouse Item 3 ("I seek others' opinions before making up my own mind")

### Response Scale

| Attribute | Reference Document | ALQ (Walumbwa) | Northouse Self-Assessment | Match? |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Scale points | 5-point (0-4) | 5-point (0-4) | 5-point (1-5) | Matches ALQ, not Northouse |

The response scale in the reference document matches the **ALQ (Walumbwa)** format (0-4, frequency-based anchors), not the Northouse version (1-5, agreement-based anchors).

### Scoring Method

| Attribute | Reference Document | ALQ (Walumbwa) | Northouse Self-Assessment | Match? |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Method | Average per subscale | Average per subscale | Sum per subscale (4-20 range) | Matches ALQ |
| Overall score | Average of all 16 items | Average | Sum (16-80 range) | Matches ALQ |
| Range | 0-4 per subscale | 0-4 per subscale | 4-20 per subscale | Matches ALQ |

### Verdict: ALQ

- **Overall alignment:** MODERATE -- the instrument is a hybrid
- **What it is:** A self-assessment adaptation that draws item content primarily from the Northouse version but applies the ALQ's response scale (0-4), scoring method (average), and uneven subscale distribution (4-5-4-3)
- **Critical discrepancies:** (1) Items are regrouped sequentially by subscale instead of interleaved; (2) Some items are reworded from the Northouse originals; (3) The 5-item Relational Transparency and 3-item Balanced Processing assignment matches the ALQ structure, not the Northouse 4-4-4-4 structure; (4) Two items ("I say exactly what I mean," "I tell the truth even when it is not easy") appear to be additions not present in the standard Northouse questionnaire; one Northouse item ("I rarely present a 'false' front to others," Item 12) and another ("Other people know where I stand on controversial issues," Item 10) appear to have been replaced
- **Scoring:** Internally consistent with the 0-4 average method
- **Recommendation:** Document this as a "self-assessment adaptation based on Walumbwa et al. (2008) four-factor model" rather than citing it as either the ALQ or the Northouse questionnaire directly. The construct validity of the four factors is well-established; the specific item adaptations are reasonable for a self-assessment context.

## 3. Session 15: CTDA -- Critical Thinking Disposition Assessment

### Source Verification

- **Based on:** Facione, P. A. (1990), The Delphi Report; Facione, P. A., Facione, N. C., & Sanchez, C. A. (1994)
- **Published Delphi Report:** American Philosophical Association, 1990
- **Published CCTDI:** Facione, Facione, & Sanchez (1994), Journal of Nursing Education, 33, 345-350
- **Reference doc citation:** CONFIRMED correct
- **Note:** The CCTDI (California Critical Thinking Disposition Inventory) is the established instrument based on the Delphi constructs. It is a 75-item proprietary instrument (Insight Assessment). The reference document uses a 20-item "adapted" version.

### Delphi Consensus Constructs Verification

The 1990 Delphi Report identified **seven** dispositional aspects of critical thinking:

| # | Delphi Construct | In CCTDI? | In Reference Doc CTDA? | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Truth-Seeking | YES | YES | Included |
| 2 | Open-Mindedness | YES | YES | Included |
| 3 | Analyticity | YES | YES | Included |
| 4 | Systematicity | YES | YES | Included |
| 5 | Inquisitiveness | YES | YES | Included |
| 6 | CT Self-Confidence | YES | **NO** | OMITTED from reference doc |
| 7 | Cognitive Maturity | YES | **NO** | OMITTED from reference doc |

**DISCREPANCY FOUND -- Missing Subscales:**
The Delphi Report identified **seven** dispositions, and the CCTDI measures all seven. The reference document CTDA includes only **five** of the seven, omitting **CT Self-Confidence** (trust in one's own reasoning) and **Cognitive Maturity** (disposition toward reflective judgment). This is a deliberate selection, not an error -- the five chosen constructs are the most relevant to the curriculum's focus on information literacy and AI-era research skills. However, it should be documented that this is a partial adaptation, not a full representation of the Delphi consensus.

### Item Content Verification

The CCTDI is a proprietary instrument (Insight Assessment), so exact item-by-item comparison is not possible through public sources. However, the reference document items can be evaluated for **face validity** against the Delphi construct definitions:

**Open-Mindedness (Items 1-4):**
- "I am willing to change my mind when evidence suggests I should." -- Consistent with Delphi definition: "tolerant of divergent views"
- "I try to consider all sides of an issue before making a judgment." -- Consistent
- "I respect the right of others to hold opinions different from mine." -- Consistent
- "I find it easy to reconsider a position when presented with good arguments." -- Consistent

**Analyticity (Items 5-8):**
- "I think through complex problems step by step." -- Consistent with Delphi: "alert to potentially problematic situations"
- "I pay close attention to details when evaluating an argument." -- Consistent
- "I prefer situations where I need to use careful reasoning." -- Consistent
- "I enjoy solving problems that require deep analysis." -- Consistent

**Systematicity (Items 9-12):**
- "I approach problems in an orderly and organized way." -- Consistent with Delphi: "organized, orderly and focused inquiry"
- "I follow a logical process when making important decisions." -- Consistent
- "I plan my approach before starting complex tasks." -- Consistent
- "I keep track of the steps I take when solving a problem." -- Consistent

**Truth-Seeking (Items 13-16):**
- "I want to know the truth even when it is uncomfortable." -- Consistent with Delphi: "eager to seek the truth"
- "I question information even when it comes from an authority figure." -- Consistent
- "I am willing to investigate claims rather than accept them at face value." -- Consistent
- "I prefer evidence over opinions when forming my views." -- Consistent

**Inquisitiveness (Items 17-20):**
- "I am curious about how things work." -- Consistent with Delphi: "intellectual curiosity"
- "I enjoy learning new things even when they are not directly useful to me." -- Consistent
- "I ask questions frequently to deepen my understanding." -- Consistent
- "I actively seek out new knowledge and experiences." -- Consistent

**Assessment:** All 20 items demonstrate strong face validity against the Delphi construct definitions. The items are clearly written and appropriate for the target population (young adults in a developmental program).

### Scoring Method

| Attribute | Reference Document | CCTDI (Published) | Match? |
|---|---|---|---|
| Subscale scoring | Average per subscale | Sum per subscale (10-60 range per subscale) | DIFFERENT |
| Overall scoring | Average of all 20 items | Sum of all 75 items | DIFFERENT (expected given different item count) |
| Reverse-scored items | None | Some items are reverse-scored | POSSIBLE DISCREPANCY |
| Range per subscale | 1-6 | 10-60 | Different due to different method |

**Note:** The CCTDI uses sum scoring with cutoffs at 40 (neutral) per subscale and 280 (neutral) overall. The reference document uses average scoring, which is reasonable for a 4-item-per-subscale instrument and produces interpretable ranges (1-6).

### Verdict: CTDA

- **Overall alignment:** REASONABLE as an adapted instrument
- **What it is:** A custom 20-item adaptation drawing on the Delphi Report constructs, not the CCTDI itself. This should be presented as "based on Facione (1990) Delphi Report constructs" rather than as the CCTDI.
- **Critical discrepancies:** (1) Only 5 of 7 Delphi constructs are represented (CT Self-Confidence and Cognitive Maturity omitted); (2) Items are newly written, not drawn from the CCTDI; (3) 20 items instead of 75
- **Strengths:** Items have strong face validity against Delphi definitions; 6-point scale matches CCTDI format; subscale structure is sound
- **Recommendation:** The reference document already correctly labels this as "Adapted from Facione" and "based on Delphi consensus constructs," which is accurate. Consider adding a note that two Delphi constructs (CT Self-Confidence, Cognitive Maturity) were intentionally excluded. The 20-item instrument should be treated as a **screening tool** rather than a comprehensive disposition measure. Consider conducting a pilot study to validate the internal consistency of the 4-item subscales.

2. **CDSE-SF Response Scale (Session 13):** Document the intentional use of 0-4 scale (vs published 1-5). The 0-4 scale produces a clean 0-100 total range, which may be preferred for platform display, but it departs from the published convention.

3. **CDSE-SF Item Ordering (Session 13):** The published CDSE-SF interleaves items across subscales. The reference document groups them sequentially. Document whether this is intentional.

4. **ALQ Hybrid Nature (Session 14):** The instrument blends Northouse self-assessment item content with ALQ response scale and scoring. Consider clarifying the instrument description as "self-assessment adaptation based on Walumbwa et al. (2008) authentic leadership model."

5. **CTDA Missing Constructs (Session 15):** Two of seven Delphi constructs (CT Self-Confidence, Cognitive Maturity) are omitted. This is defensible but should be documented.

### Items Confirmed Correct

- All four instruments have the correct number of total items
- All subscale names and definitions align with published constructs
- Scoring methods (sum vs average) are internally consistent
- No reverse-scored items are claimed where none should exist
- Interpretation bands are reasonable for the target population
- All four instruments are appropriate choices for their respective session themes
