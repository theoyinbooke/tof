### Item Discrepancies — Detailed Analysis

**Items 4 and 8 appear to be swapped or reworded:**
- The reference doc's Item 4 ("I am striving to perform better than others in my class or peer group") is close to the published Item 8 ("My goal is to perform better than the other students"), while the reference doc's Item 8 ("My aim is to perform well relative to other students") matches the published Item 4. Both items are in the Performance-Approach subscale, so the subscale assignment is correct in both cases — but the item ordering and exact wording differ.

**Items 1 and 7 — contextual adaptation:**
- The reference doc uses "my studies" instead of "my class" (Item 1) and "my most difficult class" (Item 7). This appears to be a deliberate adaptation for a non-classroom context (the foundation serves working students and trainees). This is a common and acceptable practice.

**Item 9 — minor word swap:**
- "My aim" (reference doc) vs. "My goal" (published). Both are functionally equivalent.

**Item 11 — significant rewording:**
- Reference doc: "My goal is to avoid not learning all that I can."
- Published: "I am striving to avoid an incomplete understanding of the course material."
- These express similar concepts but use substantially different wording. The reference doc version is a closer paraphrase but not the published original.

**Item 12 — moderate rewording:**
- Reference doc: "My goal is to avoid performing poorly compared to my peers."
- Published: "My aim is to avoid doing worse than other students."
- Similar meaning but different phrasing and word choice.

### Additional Published Details

The original paper notes:
- A score of **10 or greater** is the recommended clinical screening cutoff for GAD, with sensitivity of 89% and specificity of 82%.
- A higher cutoff of **15 or greater** maximizes specificity (approximately 9% prevalence), though with lower sensitivity (48%).
- Some sources cite a cutoff of **8 or greater** as optimizing sensitivity (92%) and specificity (76%) for identifying probable GAD cases.
- The GAD-7 is a **screening instrument**, not a diagnostic tool.

The reference document correctly notes: "The GAD-7 is a screening tool, not a diagnostic instrument. Scores >= 10 suggest clinically significant anxiety that warrants further evaluation."
