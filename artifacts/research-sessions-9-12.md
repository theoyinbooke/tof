## 1. Short Grit Scale (Grit-S) -- Session 9: Be a Doer

### Instrument Overview

- **Authors (published):** Angela L. Duckworth & Patrick D. Quinn
- **Published:** 2009, *Journal of Personality Assessment*, 91(2), 166-174
- **Structure:** 8 items, 5-point Likert scale, 2 subscales
- **Public availability:** Freely available from angeladuckworth.com/grit-scale

### Item Verification

The published Grit-S items in their canonical order (verified via the original instrument on PsyToolkit, the SJDM Decision Making Inventory, and the Arab Psychology Scales Database):

| # | Published Item Text | Subscale | Reverse-Scored |
|:-:|---|---|:-:|
| 1 | New ideas and projects sometimes distract me from previous ones. | Consistency of Interest | Yes |
| 2 | Setbacks don't discourage me. | Perseverance of Effort | No |
| 3 | I have been obsessed with a certain idea or project for a short time but later lost interest. | Consistency of Interest | Yes |
| 4 | I am a hard worker. | Perseverance of Effort | No |
| 5 | I often set a goal but later choose to pursue a different one. | Consistency of Interest | Yes |
| 6 | I have difficulty maintaining my focus on projects that take more than a few months to complete. | Consistency of Interest | Yes |
| 7 | I finish whatever I begin. | Perseverance of Effort | No |
| 8 | I am diligent. | Perseverance of Effort | No |

### Response Scale Verification

| Value | Published Label | Reference Doc Label |
|:-:|---|---|
| 1 | Not like me at all | Not at all like me |
| 2 | Not much like me | Not much like me |
| 3 | Somewhat like me | Somewhat like me |
| 4 | Mostly like me | Mostly like me |
| 5 | Very much like me | Very much like me |

**Finding:** Minor wording difference at anchor 1: published uses "Not like me at all" vs. reference document's "Not at all like me." Functionally equivalent. Anchors 2-5 match exactly.

### Discrepancies Found

#### DISCREPANCY 1: Item 2 Wording (MINOR)

- **Reference document:** "Setbacks don't discourage me. I don't give up easily."
- **Published original:** "Setbacks don't discourage me."

The reference document appends "I don't give up easily" to Item 2. The published Grit-S contains only "Setbacks don't discourage me." The appended clause appears to come from the original 12-item Grit Scale (Grit-O), not the 8-item Grit-S.

**Recommendation:** Use only "Setbacks don't discourage me." to match the validated Grit-S.

#### DISCREPANCY 2: Item 3 Wording (SIGNIFICANT)

- **Reference document Item 3:** "I often set a goal but later choose to pursue a different one."
- **Published original Item 3:** "I have been obsessed with a certain idea or project for a short time but later lost interest."

The reference document has swapped Items 3 and 5. The published Item 3 is "I have been obsessed with a certain idea or project for a short time but later lost interest," while the reference places "I often set a goal but later choose to pursue a different one" at position 3.

**Recommendation:** Reorder to match published item numbering. Both items belong to Consistency of Interest and are reverse-scored, so subscale scoring is unaffected, but item numbering should match the validated instrument for fidelity.

#### DISCREPANCY 3: Item 5 Wording (SIGNIFICANT -- corollary of above)

- **Reference document Item 5:** "I have difficulty maintaining my focus on projects that take more than a few months to complete."
- **Published original Item 5:** "I often set a goal but later choose to pursue a different one."

The reference document places the focus/difficulty item at position 5, but the published instrument has it at position 6.

#### DISCREPANCY 4: Item 6 vs. Item 7 Ordering

- **Reference document Item 6:** "I finish whatever I begin." (PE)
- **Published original Item 6:** "I have difficulty maintaining my focus on projects that take more than a few months to complete." (CI)
- **Reference document Item 7:** "My interests change from year to year." (CI)
- **Published original Item 7:** "I finish whatever I begin." (PE)

The reference document's Item 7 ("My interests change from year to year") does NOT appear in the published Grit-S. This item is from the original 12-item Grit-O scale. The Grit-S replaced it.

**Recommendation:** Remove "My interests change from year to year" and use the correct published Item 7: "I finish whatever I begin."

#### DISCREPANCY 5: Item 8 Wording (MINOR)

- **Reference document Item 8:** "I am diligent. I never give up."
- **Published original Item 8:** "I am diligent."

The reference appends "I never give up." The published Grit-S item is simply "I am diligent."

**Recommendation:** Use only "I am diligent." to match the validated instrument.

#### DISCREPANCY 6: Reverse-Scored Items

- **Reference document:** Items 1, 3, 5, 7 are reverse-scored.
- **Published original:** Items 1, 3, 5, 6 are reverse-scored (all Consistency of Interest items). Items 2, 4, 7, 8 are the Perseverance of Effort items (NOT reverse-scored).

The reference document states items 1, 3, 5, 7 are reverse-scored. In the published instrument, the reverse-scored items are the four Consistency of Interest items: 1, 3, 5, 6. Because the reference has rearranged items, the reverse-scored item numbers differ. However, the underlying logic is consistent: all Consistency of Interest items are reverse-scored.

**Note:** The PsyToolkit source marks Perseverance items (2, 4, 7, 8) as "reverse" because it uses a reverse-keyed response format where 5 = "Very much like me" for those items. In the standard scoring described by Duckworth, the CI items (negative wording) are reverse-scored. The reference document's approach of reverse-scoring items 1, 3, 5, 7 is based on its own reordering and includes Item 7 ("My interests change from year to year") which is not in the published Grit-S.

### Scoring Verification

- **Subscale computation (average):** CONFIRMED. Both the published instrument and reference document use the average method.
- **Consistency of Interest:** Average of 4 CI items (after reversing). Range: 1-5. CONFIRMED.
- **Perseverance of Effort:** Average of 4 PE items. Range: 1-5. CONFIRMED.
- **Overall Grit:** Average of all 8 items (after reversing). Range: 1-5. CONFIRMED.

### Interpretation Guide Verification

The reference document's severity bands are reasonable but are not standardized cutoffs from the original Duckworth & Quinn (2009) paper, which does not prescribe fixed interpretive bands. The bands used in the reference (4.1-5.0 Very gritty, 3.5-4.0 Gritty, etc.) are commonly used adaptations and are acceptable for programmatic use.

## 2. Entrepreneurial Self-Efficacy Scale (ESE) -- Session 10: Entrepreneurship

### Instrument Overview

- **Authors (reference document):** Alex F. De Noble, Don Jung, Sanford B. Ehrlich (1999)
- **Published:** 1999, *Frontiers of Entrepreneurship Research*, Babson College
- **Structure claimed in reference:** 23 items, 5-point scale, 5 subscales (Marketing, Innovation, Management, Risk-Taking, Financial Control)

### Critical Finding: Attribution Discrepancy

**This is the most significant discrepancy found in this review.**

The reference document attributes the ESE scale with 5 subscales (Marketing, Innovation, Management, Risk-Taking, Financial Control) to De Noble, Jung, & Ehrlich (1999). However, extensive web research reveals that:

1. **De Noble, Jung, & Ehrlich (1999)** developed an ESE scale with **6 subscales** (not 5):
   - Developing New Product and Market Opportunities (7 items)
   - Building an Innovative Environment (4 items)
   - Initiating Investor Relationships (3 items)
   - Defining Core Purpose (3 items)
   - Coping with Unexpected Challenges (3 items)
   - Developing Critical Human Resources (3 items)
   - Total: 23 items across 6 dimensions

2. **The 5-subscale structure** (Marketing, Innovation, Management, Risk-Taking, Financial Control) actually comes from **Chen, Greene, & Crick (1998)**, published in the *Journal of Business Venturing*, 13(4), 295-316:
   - Marketing (6 items)
   - Innovation (4 items)
   - Management (5 items)
   - Risk-Taking (4 items)
   - Financial Control (3 items)
   - Total: 22 items across 5 dimensions

**The reference document appears to have conflated the De Noble (1999) authorship with the Chen et al. (1998) subscale structure and item content.**

### Item Verification

The 23 items in the reference document, with their "I can..." stem format and the five subscales (Marketing, Innovation, Management, Risk-Taking, Financial Control), are consistent with the **Chen, Greene, & Crick (1998)** scale tradition, though the reference document's item count (23) is one more than the original Chen et al. scale (22). The reference document appears to be using an adapted/expanded version of the Chen et al. scale rather than the De Noble et al. scale.

The item content itself (e.g., "I can identify the need for a new product or service," "I can design a product or service that will satisfy customer needs") is consistent with the type of items used in ESE research in this tradition. However, because the De Noble (1999) original paper was a conference proceeding and not as widely reproduced as Chen et al. (1998), exact item-by-item verification against the De Noble original is limited.

### Subscale Item Counts

| Subscale (Reference Doc) | Items | Chen et al. (1998) Count | Match? |
|---|:-:|:-:|:-:|
| Marketing | 5 (items 1-5) | 6 | Close but -1 |
| Innovation | 7 (items 6-12) | 4 | Significant difference (+3) |
| Management | 4 (items 13-16) | 5 | Close but -1 |
| Risk-Taking | 4 (items 17-20) | 4 | Match |
| Financial Control | 3 (items 21-23) | 3 | Match |
| **Total** | **23** | **22** | **+1 item** |

The reference document's item distribution does not match Chen et al. (1998) exactly, nor does it match De Noble et al. (1999)'s six-factor structure. It appears to be a **custom adaptation** drawing on both instruments.

### Response Scale Verification

- **Reference document:** 5-point scale (1 = Completely disagree to 5 = Completely agree)
- **Chen et al. (1998):** 5-point Likert scale
- **De Noble et al. (1999):** 5-point Likert scale

The response scale format is consistent with both original instruments.

### Scoring Verification

- **Average method for subscales and overall:** Consistent with standard ESE scoring practices in the literature.
- **No reverse-scored items:** Consistent with both Chen et al. and De Noble et al.

### Interpretation Guide Verification

The severity bands (4.0-5.0 High, 3.0-3.9 Moderate, 2.0-2.9 Low, 1.0-1.9 Very Low) are reasonable programmatic thresholds, not derived from a specific published norm. This is acceptable for a non-clinical educational context.

### Summary of Action Items for ESE

| Issue | Severity | Action Required |
|---|:-:|---|
| Attribution error: 5 subscales attributed to De Noble (1999) but match Chen et al. (1998) | Significant | Correct attribution OR explicitly note the scale is an adaptation |
| De Noble (1999) has 6 subscales, not 5 | Significant | If using De Noble, restructure to 6 subscales; if using Chen et al., correct the citation |
| Item count (23) does not match either Chen (22) or De Noble (23 items, 6 subscales) | Moderate | Clarify source and reconcile item count |
| Individual item wording could not be fully verified against the De Noble (1999) conference paper | Moderate | Obtain original De Noble paper to verify, or re-attribute to Chen et al. (1998) |

### Recommendation

The reference document should either:

1. **Correct the attribution** to Chen, Greene, & Crick (1998) and adjust item count/subscales to match, OR
2. **Explicitly describe the scale as an adaptation** drawing from both De Noble et al. (1999) and Chen et al. (1998), with clear documentation of which items were modified or added, OR
3. **Restructure to the De Noble (1999) six-factor model** if the intention was to use that instrument.

### References

- Chen, C. C., Greene, P. G., & Crick, A. (1998). Does entrepreneurial self-efficacy distinguish entrepreneurs from managers? *Journal of Business Venturing*, 13(4), 295-316.
- McGee, J. E., Peterson, M., Mueller, S. L., & Sequeira, J. M. (2009). Entrepreneurial self-efficacy: Refining the measure. *Entrepreneurship Theory and Practice*, 33(4), 965-988.

## 3. Servant Leadership Scale -- Short Form (SL-7) -- Session 11: The Path to Greatness

### Instrument Overview

- **Authors (reference document):** Robert C. Liden, Sandy J. Wayne, Hao Zhao, David Henderson
- **Published (reference document):** 2015, *The Leadership Quarterly*, 26(2), 254-269
- **Actual authors of 2015 paper:** Robert C. Liden, Sandy J. Wayne, Jeremy D. Meuser, Jia Hu, Junfeng Wu, & Chenwei Liao
- **Original SL-28 authors (2008):** Liden, Wayne, Zhao, & Henderson
- **Structure:** 7 items, 7-point scale, each item = 1 dimension

### Author Attribution Discrepancy

The reference document lists the SL-7 authors as "Robert C. Liden, Sandy J. Wayne, Hao Zhao, David Henderson." However:

- **Liden, Wayne, Zhao, & Henderson** are the authors of the **2008 SL-28 paper** (the original 28-item scale).
- **Liden, Wayne, Meuser, Hu, Wu, & Liao** are the authors of the **2015 SL-7 validation paper**.

**Recommendation:** Update the author attribution to reflect the correct 2015 paper authors, or note that the SL-7 was developed from Liden, Wayne, Zhao, & Henderson's (2008) SL-28.

### Item Verification

The original SL-7 items (other-rated, verified via a published validation study in the South African Journal of Economic and Management Sciences, Scielo):

| # | Original SL-7 Item (Other-Rated) | Dimension | Reference Doc Item (Self-Adapted) |
|:-:|---|---|---|
| 1 | My leader can tell if something work-related is going wrong. | Conceptual Skills | I can tell when something is going wrong before others notice. |
| 2 | My leader makes my career development a priority. | Helping Subordinates Grow | I make the personal development of others a priority. |
| 3 | I would seek help from my leader if I had a personal problem. | Emotional Healing | I care about the emotional well-being of others around me. |
| 4 | My leader emphasises the importance of giving back to the community. | Creating Value for Community | I emphasize the importance of giving back to the community. |
| 5 | My leader puts my best interests ahead of his or her own. | Putting Subordinates First | I put others' best interests ahead of my own. |
| 6 | My leader gives me the freedom to handle difficult situations in the way that I feel is best. | Empowering | I give others the freedom to handle difficult situations in their own way. |
| 7 | My leader would NOT compromise ethical principles in order to achieve success. | Behaving Ethically | I hold myself to high ethical standards in everything I do. |

### Item-by-Item Analysis

**Item 1 (Conceptual Skills):**
- Original: "My leader can tell if something work-related is going wrong."
- Adapted: "I can tell when something is going wrong before others notice."
- Assessment: Reasonable self-adaptation. Adds "before others notice" which slightly shifts meaning beyond the original.

**Item 2 (Helping Subordinates Grow):**
- Original: "My leader makes my career development a priority."
- Adapted: "I make the personal development of others a priority."
- Assessment: Good adaptation. Broadens from "career development" to "personal development."

**Item 3 (Emotional Healing):**
- Original: "I would seek help from my leader if I had a personal problem."
- Adapted: "I care about the emotional well-being of others around me."
- Assessment: Significant rewrite. The original measures willingness to seek help from a leader (follower's trust); the adaptation measures caring about others' well-being. These are substantially different constructs.

**Item 4 (Creating Value for Community):**
- Original: "My leader emphasises the importance of giving back to the community."
- Adapted: "I emphasize the importance of giving back to the community."
- Assessment: Direct, clean adaptation. Matches well.

**Item 5 (Putting Subordinates First):**
- Original: "My leader puts my best interests ahead of his or her own."
- Adapted: "I put others' best interests ahead of my own."
- Assessment: Good, direct adaptation.

**Item 6 (Empowering):**
- Original: "My leader gives me the freedom to handle difficult situations in the way that I feel is best."
- Adapted: "I give others the freedom to handle difficult situations in their own way."
- Assessment: Good, direct adaptation.

**Item 7 (Behaving Ethically):**
- Original: "My leader would NOT compromise ethical principles in order to achieve success."
- Adapted: "I hold myself to high ethical standards in everything I do."
- Assessment: Significant rewrite. The original is specific to non-compromise of ethics for success; the adaptation is a broader self-assessment of ethical standards.

### Item Order Discrepancy

The reference document orders items as:
1. Emotional Healing, 2. Creating Value for Community, 3. Conceptual Skills, 4. Empowering, 5. Helping Subordinates Grow, 6. Putting Subordinates First, 7. Behaving Ethically

The published SL-7 orders items as:
1. Conceptual Skills, 2. Helping Subordinates Grow, 3. Emotional Healing, 4. Creating Value for Community, 5. Putting Subordinates First, 6. Empowering, 7. Behaving Ethically

The item ordering differs substantially. Since each item is scored independently as its own dimension, this does not affect scoring, but it differs from the published sequence.

### Response Scale Verification

| Value | Published Scale | Reference Doc Scale |
|:-:|---|---|
| 1 | Strongly disagree | Strongly disagree |
| 2 | Disagree | Disagree |
| 3 | Slightly disagree | Somewhat disagree |
| 4 | Neutral | Neither agree nor disagree |
| 5 | Slightly agree | Somewhat agree |
| 6 | Agree | Agree |
| 7 | Strongly agree | Strongly agree |

**Finding:** Anchors 3, 4, and 5 differ slightly. "Slightly disagree/agree" vs. "Somewhat disagree/agree" and "Neutral" vs. "Neither agree nor disagree." These are functionally equivalent but technically different from the published scale.

### Scoring Verification

- **Average of all 7 items, range 1-7:** CONFIRMED. Matches published scoring.
- **No reverse-scored items:** CONFIRMED.
- **Individual item analysis for dimension-level insight:** CONFIRMED. Appropriate use of the scale.

### Interpretation Guide Verification

The reference document's bands (5.5-7.0 High, 4.0-5.4 Moderate, 2.5-3.9 Low, 1.0-2.4 Very Low) are programmatic thresholds, not published norms from the original paper. This is acceptable for programmatic use but should be noted as custom thresholds.
